Overview

Codalyzer is a tool designed to analyze the impact that codecs have on WAV and FLAC files. It features an analysis window displaying the most important parameters to evaluate during the encoding process. Values such as True Peak, Peak, RMS, etc., are measured before and after the conversion process.

It also includes a very comprehensive Waveform window where you can visualize the signal levels with and without loss, making it possible to identify potential clipping in the lossy versions — and all of this down to sample level.

It is a tool intended for mastering engineers to evaluate the degradations that their original masters may suffer when subjected to lossy codecs.
